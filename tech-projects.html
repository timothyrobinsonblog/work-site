Here are three tech projects that I’ve worked on over the past several years: one built for a start-up, a personal creative site, and a design and development practice that I co-founded.
atomic vision ►
I co-founded Atomic Vision to offer personalised yet affordable web design and development services tailored to small businesses competing in a crowded marketplace. I’ve designed and developed a suite of pre-configured, modular websites from scratch primarily using JavaScript, HTML, CSS, and the Adobe Creative Suite. Built with reusable code modules and pluggable features to enable rapid deployment. I also conducted target market research and developed business strategy. Launch is imminent.

plastic words ►
Plastic Words was a corporate communications start-up based in Perth, Australia. I collaborated with the team on client communications, product development, and brand strategy. I also led the design, development, and maintenance of the company’s main website, built to showcase our brand to prospective clients. I created this responsive website with dark/light modes and a corporate-minimalist aesthetic. I also produced infographics, business cards, Google Meet backgrounds, and developed an interactive word game for potential marketing content. I worked independently and remotely with a high degree of autonomy on all projects.

on the road ►
Remember building in Dreamweaver using a dial-up modem? Years after that first brush with development, I finally learnt to code during the pandemic. This site was my first real project after a crash course in the basics. I built it to showcase my creative work, primarily photography, and front-end development skills. At the time, it felt like such a steep learning curve, and there was no AI to lean on. Just Stack Exchange, tutorials, and the docs. My persistence paid off though, and I’m still proud of how it turned out. It remains a strong reflection of some early lessons in tech, and the photography that I did during my extensive travels.
